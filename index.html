Research Project Overview

This project by Pratham Gulliya investigates the tangible financial impact of ESG in M&A. This section provides an overview of the project's strategic value, key research questions, and the methodology used to derive the findings.

Why This Project Is Important

  • Investment Banking Relevance: It focuses on M&A strategy and valuation modeling, which are core skills for investment banking roles.
  • Differentiator: It goes beyond basic DCF and LBO models to explore strategic premiums and non-financial drivers of value, offering a unique perspective.
  • ESG Trend: The project is highly topical, addressing how the growing ESG trend is shaping modern dealmaking and corporate strategy.
  • Publishable Quality: The research fits journals in finance, ESG policy, and international business, demonstrating a high level of rigor.

Core Research Objectives

  • Do acquirers pay valuation premiums in ESG-related M&A deals?
  • Is there a difference in premium in Indian vs. global markets?
  • Does ESG alignment lead to post-merger performance improvements?
  • What sectors (e.g., energy, tech, manufacturing) show the largest premiums?

Research Methodology

The study used a rigorous, multi-faceted approach to analyze the data from a dataset of ESG-driven M&A deals in India and globally.

  • Dataset & Variables: The dataset was sourced from providers like **Refinitiv, Bloomberg, and PitchBook**. We defined ESG relevance based on acquirer/target’s ESG ratings or SDG-aligned sectors. Key variables included the **Deal Premium** (Offer Price − Target Stock Price), ESG Score, Target/Acquirer Sector, Deal Size, and broader Market Conditions.
  • Models: We employed **OLS Regression** to analyze the relationship between the premium and ESG scores. **Difference-in-Differences** was used to study post-merger performance, and an **Event Study** was performed to measure short-term returns post-announcement.

The ESG-Integrated M&A Playbook

Successful modern M&A requires embedding ESG considerations across the entire deal lifecycle. It has evolved from a final check to a continuous thread influencing strategy, valuation, and long-term value capture.

1. Deal Sourcing & Origination

Proactively screen targets for ESG alignment and undervalued ESG assets, moving beyond purely financial metrics.

2. Valuation & Due Diligence

4. Post-Merger Integration

Develop plans to capture ESG synergies, harmonize reporting, and enhance the combined entity's overall ESG performance, which leads to measurable improvements in post-merger performance.
